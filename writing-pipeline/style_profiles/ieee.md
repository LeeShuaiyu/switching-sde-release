# Style Profile: IEEE-like

## Use when

The target is an engineering or systems-oriented venue that values precise setup, reproducible experiments, and efficient technical exposition.

## Writing traits

- concise and formal
- short to medium paragraphs
- explicit descriptions of setup, metrics, and implementation details
- restrained claims
- direct statement of what improved, under what conditions, and by how much

## Emphasis

- technical clarity
- disciplined notation
- experiment protocol
- fair comparison
- limitations stated plainly
- figures and tables that are directly tied to claims

## Avoid

- inflated novelty language
- overly literary transitions
- vague claims like "substantially better" without context
- code-style identifiers or private project nicknames as narrative terminology
- unexplained visuals

## Section rhythm

- state the setup early
- present results cleanly
- discuss why the method helps after evidence appears
